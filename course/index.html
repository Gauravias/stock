What IS STOCK MARKET

The stock market refers to the collection of exchanges and platforms where buying, selling, and issuing shares of publicly traded companies take place. It is a marketplace where investors can trade stocks, which represent ownership in those companies.

What IS TRADINF

Trading in the stock market refers to the buying and selling of stocks or other securities with the intention of making a profit. Traders engage in various strategies to take advantage of price movements in the market, aiming to buy stocks at a lower price and sell them at a higher price or vice versa.

What is Short-term vs. Long-term Trading:

Trading can be categorized into short-term and long-term approaches. Short-term traders, often referred to as day traders or swing traders, hold positions for a short period, ranging from minutes to a few weeks. Long-term traders, such as investors or position traders, hold stocks for a longer duration, often years, based on a belief in the company's potential growth.

Technical Analysis

Traders often use technical analysis to study historical price patterns, trading volumes, and various technical indicators to identify potential buying or selling opportunities.

What is Fundamental Analysis

raders may also consider fundamental analysis, which involves evaluating a company's financial health, earnings, industry trends, and other relevant factors to assess the intrinsic value of a stock.

Market Orders vs. Limit Orders

Traders can place market orders to buy or sell a stock at the prevailing market price, or they can use limit orders to specify the maximum price they are willing to pay to buy or the minimum price they are willing to accept to sell.

Investing in the stock market

Investing in the stock market carries risks, as stock prices can be volatile and subject to various factors such as economic conditions, company performance, and market sentiment. Investors often conduct research, analyze financial data, and seek advice to make informed investment decisions in the stock market.
